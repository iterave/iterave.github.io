Practices

There's no single right methodology for everyone. Together we can discover the methodology that's right for your company.

  • Lean

    A company wide commitment.

    Lean is a people focused set of principles that helps create a system through which people can succeed. Specific practices will help eliminate waste, focus on quality and reduce cycle time. However, Lean can be most successful when adopted more broadly throughout a company.

  • Scrum

    Simple, project-level practices.

    Scrum has gained immense popularity due to its simplicity and strong commercial support. Alone it's not enough, but together with support from other methodologies such as Lean and XP, Scrum can add key benefits such as greater visibility and more collaboration at a product management and project management level.

  • Extreme Programming

    Highly disciplined software development.

    At the development level, XP focuses on creating rock solid software that is fast to build, easy to maintain, well-tested and with automated processes wherever possible to reduce costs and complexity.

  • Your Methodology

    Nobody can tell you what your methodology is...

    The methods mentioned above were each created with experience and input from people at all levels and roles within the software development community. And there are others still! While they each differ, they all share common themes that can be explored and integrated into your methodology.

Open-Source Software

Reduce or eliminate recurring licensing costs.

Open-source software has become the foundation of the Internet and the world's most successful companies. You can apply an open-source strategy throughout your entire company, or isolate it only to certain parts. You can integrate it with commercial solutions, or use it to delay large investments in commercial software. Using good software development practices, you can easily build your entire product using open-source solutions, and then switch to a commercial product if you ever feel the need to do so. However, a more popular option is to buy a support agreement from a third party vendor who specializes in services for open-source software. This keeps your options open, and avoids vendor lock-in.

Cloud Infrastructure

Virtually infinite scalability.

For most companies, managing a data center, or even just a server room, is not a core competency. It is a non-functional requirement - a dependency. It's no more of a competitive advantage than lights are. Now you can manage infrastructure as a utility, just like electricity. You can leave the details to the best infrastructure engineers in the world and leverage an almost inifinite pool of computing resources that can scale up or down with your company as needed. Amazon Web Services is the leader in this space and the best choice for most companies. But for certain specialized needs, there are alternatives that provide more traditional infrastructure or Canadian based data centers, in case you're data needs to remain in the country for any reason. There are many options. None should require you to refrigerate your own servers!

Automation

From code to production.

Automation is extremely important to ensuring your software is easy to maintain and support as it grows in size, complexity and functionality. Having a good automation strategy from the start, will ensure that your projects don't get bogged down with the overhead of an ever-increasing number of manual tasks. The software development process can be automated at nearly every stage within its lifecycle. From your source control system, code can be retrieved, built, tested, integrated, packaged, versioned and released to an immutable binary artifact repository. Think of it like your very own app store. From there, an automated deployment process can spin up environments and deploy your applications to test, staging, production or customer specific environments at the touch of a button.
